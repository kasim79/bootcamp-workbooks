# Summary of What We Did and What We Learned

### Day 1 – Understanding Data Laws and Regulations  
We began the course by learning about the key laws that govern how organisations handle customer data. This included the Data Protection Act, GDPR, the Freedom of Information Act, and the Computer Misuse Act. We explored what each law means, why it is important, and how it affects everyday work with data. This helped build a strong foundation in ethical and legal responsibilities, as well as the consequences of mishandling information.

---

### Day 2 – Building Core Excel Skills  
We worked with the retail sales dataset to develop essential Excel skills. This included converting raw data into a structured table, sorting values, and using formulas such as SUM and AVERAGE. These tasks strengthened confidence in navigating spreadsheets and performing basic data analysis. We also had time to explore the dataset independently, which encouraged problem‑solving and creativity with data.

---

### Day 3 – Pivot Tables, Categorisation, and Visualisation  
We learned how to use Pivot Tables to summarise and analyse larger datasets. In the Bike Sales lab, we identified patterns across markets, countries, age groups, and genders. We also used the SWITCH function to categorise sales volumes into High, Medium, and Low, which introduced logical functions and data classification. Later, we created charts and visualisations to present insights clearly and effectively.

---

### Day 4 – Presenting Insights to Senior Leaders  
We shifted from technical skills to communication and presentation. We researched how to prepare for delivering analysis to a board of directors, including planning, structuring a message, and choosing the right tools. We learned about prospecting, public‑speaking best practices, and how to communicate business issues—such as customer churn at the 12‑month renewal point—in a confident and professional way. We also evaluated different visualisation tools and considered which ones are most effective for presenting insights.

---

## Overall Learning  
Throughout Week 1, we developed a strong foundation in both data handling and professional communication:

- Understanding key data laws and ethical responsibilities  
- Building confidence with Excel tables, formulas, pivot tables, and categorisation  
- Analysing datasets and extracting meaningful insights  
- Creating visualisations to support decision‑making  
- Improving communication and presentation skills for senior audiences  

These activities prepared us for more advanced data analysis and business intelligence work in the following weeks.

# Summary of What We Did and What We Learned

### Day 1 – Cloud Computing Fundamentals  
We began Week 4 by exploring the core concepts of cloud computing and how it is used in the real world. We discussed what cloud computing can do for individuals and businesses, including scalability, cost savings, remote access, and improved collaboration. We compared cloud computing to traditional on‑premises systems and identified the advantages and limitations of each approach.  
We also researched major cloud providers such as Microsoft Azure, Amazon Web Services (AWS), and Google Cloud Platform (GCP), learning about their key features, services, and use cases. This helped us understand the broader cloud ecosystem and why organisations choose specific providers.

---

### Day 1 – Cloud Service Models  
We explored the three main cloud service models:  
- **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)**  
- **PaaS (Platform as a Service)**  
- **SaaS (Software as a Service)**  

For each model, we researched what it is, how it works, and real‑world examples of when it would be used. This helped us understand the different levels of control and responsibility businesses have depending on the service model they choose.

---

### Day 1 – Cloud Deployment Models  
We researched the four main cloud deployment models:  
- **Public Cloud**  
- **Private Cloud**  
- **Hybrid Cloud**  
- **Community Cloud**  

For each model, we explained what it is, when it is appropriate, and provided real‑world examples of organisations that might use it. This helped us understand how businesses choose the right cloud environment based on security, cost, and operational needs.

---

### Day 2 – Cybersecurity and Data Protection Laws  
We explored the **Computer Misuse Act 1990**, including the three major areas it covers: unauthorised access, unauthorised modification, and intent to commit further offences. We also researched the additional powers introduced by the **Police and Justice Act 2006**, which strengthened laws around hacking tools and cybercrime.  
We then looked at what personal data employers can legally store about employees, and what requires explicit permission. This helped us understand the importance of data privacy and compliance in the workplace.  
We also researched copyright infringement, plagiarism, and the consequences of software piracy. Finally, we matched a series of legal statements to the correct UK laws, reinforcing our understanding of digital legislation and user rights.

---

### Day 3 – Exploring Azure Data Services  
We completed three Azure labs focused on understanding how data is stored, processed, and analysed in the cloud:  
- **Lab 3: Explore relational data in Azure**  
- **Lab 4: Explore non‑relational data in Azure**  
- **Lab 5: Explore data analytics in Azure**  

These labs introduced us to Azure SQL Database, Azure Cosmos DB, and Azure Synapse Analytics. We learned how Azure handles structured and unstructured data, how to query cloud‑based databases, and how analytics tools support large‑scale data processing.

---

### Day 4 – Azure DP‑900 Practice Exam  
We completed the DP‑900 practice exam as a team, discussing each question and researching answers together. This helped reinforce our understanding of Azure data fundamentals, including storage options, analytics tools, security, and data processing concepts.

---

### Day 4 – Azure Proposal for “Paws & Whiskers”  
We completed a detailed written task where we created a cloud proposal for a fictional pet shop transitioning to Azure. This included:  
- Identifying relevant data laws such as GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018  
- Recommending Azure services for storage, analytics, and automation  
- Describing the types of data the business would work with  
- Designing a data model suitable for sales, customers, and inventory  
- Recommending data formats such as CSV, JSON, and Parquet  
- Explaining how Azure ensures data security, encryption, and compliance  
- Considering backup, disaster recovery, visualisation with Power BI, and future scalability  

This task helped us apply cloud knowledge to a realistic business scenario and understand how Azure supports modern data solutions.
